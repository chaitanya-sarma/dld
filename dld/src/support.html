FAQ
Which are the 20 states covered in this database?

Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Gujarat, Haryana, Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Odisha, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, Uttar Pradesh, West Bengal, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Uttarakhand, Assam, Himachal Pradesh, Kerala, and Telangana.

What is the difference between apportioned and Unapportioned data?

Unapportioned data includes data for all districts in the 20 states of India whereas 1966 apportioned data contains data for 1966 district boundaries only.

What are the common sources of data?

Directorate of Economics and Statistics, Directorate of Agriculture, Agriculture department, State Agricultural Marketing Board, Directorate of Veterinary Services, SLBC, NABARD, Public Works Department, Directorate of Animal Husbandry, Horticulture and Food Processing Department, Directorate of Horticulture, Department of Child and Women Development, Department of Environment &Forest.

Which environment variables are available for bulk download?

Runoff moisture and Wind speed on monthly scale are available only for bulk download.

What is the source of pixel level data?

AID data (Terra Climate dataset).

What is the source of Fertilizer data?

Fertilizer statistics from Fertilizer Association of India.
